Research

Bridging Clinical Psychology, Developmental Science, and Human-Computer Interaction

Technology is reshaping how people connect, cope, and seek support. I study what that means for social functioning, development, and well-being.

MA Thesis

Adolescent Generative AI Use and Social and Psychological Well-being

Using probability-based survey data from over 3,000 younger adolescents to provide the first population-level characterization of relational AI use — virtual friends, mental health chatbots, AI companions. Testing preregistered hypotheses about whether social disconnection predicts virtual-friend use and lower well-being predicts mental health chatbot use, drawing on social compensation and emotion regulation frameworks.

Intensive Longitudinal Study

Online Communication and Daily Peer Belonging in Early Adolescence

An ecological momentary assessment study modeling within-person daily fluctuations in adolescents' communication with close others and sense of belongingness over 14-day EMA bursts. Disaggregates relationally-targeted communication from overall digital volume to understand what specifically matters for connection — with planned expansion to capture AI-directed communication.

Computational Research

Few-Shot Classification of Help Seeking in Adolescent Texts

Developing few-shot classifiers to detect help-seeking, help-providing, and mental health content in 90 days of naturalistic adolescent typed text — over 700,000 entries and nearly 3 million words. Addresses a core methodological challenge: relevant signals are sparse (fewer than 2% of messages reference mental health) yet potentially consequential for understanding youth well-being.

Applied Project

Developer Well-Being Reflection Tool

A theory-driven journaling tool for software developers grounded in Self-Determination Theory, incorporating autonomy, competence, and relatedness alongside energy and stress metrics. Designed to support structured reflection on well-being tied to daily work experiences.

About

Over a decade of programming experience taught me that products ship faster than we can understand their effects. That background shapes how I approach research. I can read a model architecture and a clinical assessment, which lets me ask questions that sit between disciplines: not just whether adolescents use AI for companionship, but what it means developmentally when they do. I believe we don't have the evidence for whether relational AI use helps or harms young people. My work is about building that evidence.
